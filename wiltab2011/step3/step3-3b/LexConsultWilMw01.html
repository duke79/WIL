Lexical Consultation Wilson--Moinier Williams

Peter M. Scharf

15 August 2011

Under our grant from the NEH/DFG in collaboration with the University of Cologne to synthesize and revise Sanskrit lexical sources, we are integrating various lexical sources into a single digital interface. This involves first and foremost correlating the headwords in the various lexical sources. To achieve this we consider the conventions of each source and write rules to map from a canonical headword list to the list of headwords in each lexical source. We then search for every headword in each lexical source in the canonical list and vice versa. If a form is not found, we figure out why and attempt to map it to the closest corresponding form. The reasons a headword in a specific lexical source is not found in our canonical list or vice versa are various: typing errors, printing errors, variants, alternate sandhi, lack of forms with certain derivational affixes or inflectional terminations.

Our first attempt to correlate headwords is between Wilson's Sanskrit-English Dictionary and Monier Williams' Sanskrit-English Dictionary. Since Monier Williams' Dictionary (MW) is much more comprehensive, we first look up all the words in Wilson in MW. About 5,000 words were not found. About a thousand of these seemed to vary because of predictable differences and we attempted to classify those differences according to regular criteria and map the Wilson headword to the suspected match in MW. We then asked a post-doc to check each pair for accuracy. I then checked the post-doc's work. The following table contains the list of categories we used to classify the Wilson headword.

A procedural question arises as to how we should handle factual errors, i.e. those classed as W in the above list. It is my opinion that we should not correct them because it may be of historical interest to researchers that certain errors were made by certain lexicographers at certain times and places. Instead we should keep the error as is, flag it as a deprecated form, and map it to the correct form in our canonical list and other lexical sources. We should not use such forms for the purpose of generating a list of correct Sanskrit inflected forms (our full-form lexicon), and we should indicate that the form is deprecated by us in some way in the digital display. Nevertheless, I felt it adviseable to consult with you about this procedure and to present you with a few challenging examples. Please let me know whether you approve of the way I am handling these or whether you have suggestions for improvement or have an entirely different opinion of how to handle them.
